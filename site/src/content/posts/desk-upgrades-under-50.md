Most desk advice is a list of $400 chairs and $900 standing desks. Skip that. If you work at a desk every day, these seven upgrades — all under $50, most under $25 — are the ones that actually change how the day feels. In order of impact:

## 1. Laptop stand (~$20–35)
The single highest-impact upgrade if you work on a laptop. Raising the screen to eye level fixes the hunch everything else fails to fix. **Look for:** aluminum, adjustable height, folds flat. **Skip if** you already use an external monitor at eye level.

## 2. Monitor riser (~$15–30)
Same job as the laptop stand for external monitors, plus it creates storage space underneath. **Look for:** bamboo or steel, at least 4" of lift. A stack of thick books does the same job at $0 — buy this for the tidiness, not the height.

## 3. Desk pad (~$12–25)
Sounds cosmetic; isn't. A large desk pad gives your wrists a soft edge, quiets the keyboard, and makes any desk look intentional. **Look for:** 80cm+ width, stitched edges.

## 4. Clamp-on LED lamp (~$20–40)
Overhead room light puts your own shadow on your work. A clamp lamp takes zero desk space and fixes video-call lighting too. **Look for:** adjustable color temperature, a clamp (not a base).

## 5. Cable tray (~$10–20)
One screwed-under-the-desk tray removes the entire cable nest from the floor. Ten minutes of installation, permanent result. (Full walkthrough: [our 10-minute cable management guide](/posts/cable-management-10-minutes).)

## 6. Headphone hook (~$8–12)
Adhesive, takes 10 seconds to install, and your headphones stop being a desk-space tax.

## 7. Footrest (~$15–30)
The upgrade nobody believes in until they try it. If your chair is even slightly tall for you, a footrest quietly fixes the leg fatigue you blamed on the chair. **Skip if** your feet already sit flat.

## What *not* to buy first
RGB anything, desk organizers bigger than the mess they organize, and any "ergonomic" gadget over $50 before you've fixed screen height — that's the one that matters.
