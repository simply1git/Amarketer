Cable management content is dominated by people who route custom-sleeved cables through drilled channels. This is not that. This is the ten-minute version that gets you 90% of the result with three cheap items and zero drilling.

## The $20 kit

1. **Under-desk cable tray (~$12)** — screws or clamps under the back edge of the desk. Every power strip and adapter goes in it. This is the whole trick: get the pile off the floor and out of sight.
2. **Adhesive cable clips, pack of 10 (~$5)** — three on the back edge of the desk route the cables that have to travel (monitor, lamp, charger).
3. **Velcro tie roll (~$4)** — bundle the excess length instead of letting it dangle. Velcro, not zip ties — you *will* re-do this someday.

## The 10 minutes

1. Unplug everything. Yes, everything. *(2 min)*
2. Power strip into the tray, plugs back in from there. *(3 min)*
3. Clip the traveling cables along the desk's back edge. *(2 min)*
4. Velcro-bundle every slack loop. *(2 min)*
5. Look under the desk. Empty floor. *(1 min, spent feeling smug)*

## What to skip

Cable sleeves (look great, fight you on every change), in-wall routing (you rent), and any "cable management box" bigger than the tray — it's a drawer of shame that costs money.

The floor-level difference is bigger than it sounds: a clear floor means vacuuming happens, feet stretch, and the desk corner stops feeling like a server closet.
