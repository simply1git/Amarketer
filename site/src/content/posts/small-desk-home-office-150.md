You don't need a spare room, a corner office, or $1,000. A genuinely good work setup fits on a 100cm desk in the corner of a bedroom for about $150 — if you spend it in the right order.

## The budget, in priority order

| Item | Budget | Why it's on the list |
|---|---|---|
| Laptop stand | ~$25 | Screen at eye level is 80% of ergonomics |
| External keyboard + mouse | ~$30 | Required once the laptop is raised |
| Clamp-on LED lamp | ~$25 | Light without losing desk space |
| Desk pad | ~$15 | Defines the workspace, saves your wrists |
| Chair cushion + footrest | ~$40 | Turns whatever chair you own into a workable one |
| Headphone hook + cable clips | ~$15 | Keeps a small desk usable |

**Total: ~$150.** No monitor on this list on purpose — on a small desk, a raised laptop with a good keyboard beats a cramped dual-screen setup you sit sideways to.

## Placement rules that cost nothing

1. **Desk perpendicular to the window,** not facing it (screen glare) and not back to it (silhouette on video calls).
2. **Nothing on the desk you don't touch daily.** Small desks die by accumulation.
3. **Chair fully under the desk when you finish.** In a shared or small room, being able to "close" the office matters more than any purchase.

## The order matters more than the items

If you can only spend $50 this month: laptop stand, keyboard, mouse. That's the setup's spine. Everything else is comfort added one paycheck at a time — which is exactly how a one-person business should buy equipment anyway.
