# FTC Affiliate Disclosure Requirements

## Core rule

If we earn a commission from a link, the reader must be told **clearly and conspicuously, before they act on the link**. "Clear and conspicuous" (FTC 2023 update) means: plain language, hard to miss, same medium as the claim, not buried behind links, footers, or "more" folds.

## Approved site disclosure block (place before first affiliate link)

> *This post contains affiliate links. If you buy through them, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you.*

For pages with Amazon links, append the Amazon-mandated sentence:

> *As an Amazon Associate I earn from qualifying purchases.*

## Placement rules

- Top of article or immediately before the first affiliate link — never footer-only.
- Product roundups/comparison tables: disclosure above the table.
- Email: disclosure in the body before the first link.
- Social: `#ad` or "affiliate link" in the visible portion of the post.

## What is NOT sufficient (FTC guidance)

- A site-wide disclosure page linked in the footer, alone.
- Vague wording ("we may be compensated", "partner links") without saying what that means.
- Disclosure only after the links / at the end of the post.
- Hovering tooltips or fine print.

## Non-US note

If traffic becomes significantly non-US, check ASA (UK) and EU consumer-protection equivalents — generally stricter, same principles. Not yet in scope.
