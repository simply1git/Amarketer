# Pinterest Product-Roundup Engine

## Weekly production loop (mostly agent work)
1. Agent researches trending/bestseller products in the week's theme (desk setups, cable management, ergonomic gear, planners, lighting).
2. Agent drafts 2 roundup hub pages → owner approves → published to site.
3. Agent generates 10–15 pins via `make-pin.mjs` (varied titles: "under $50", "small desk edition", "wfh upgrade") with descriptions + disclosure tags ready to paste.
4. Owner posts pins (1–3/day drip, not bulk) — ~10 min/day, schedulable in one sitting weekly with Pinterest's native scheduler.

## Why this converts despite 1-4.5% rates
Volume and persistence: pins are search results, not feed posts — a pin posted once can drive clicks for a year. Amazon's 24h cookie converts on impulse-priced visual products. The math that matters: 50 live pins × long tail beats 5 perfect pins.

## Account health rules
Consistency over bursts; 80/20 promotional ratio; every pin links to our hub pages (context converts better than raw product links, per practitioner consensus); never copy others' pin images.
